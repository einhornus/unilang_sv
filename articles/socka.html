socka (34089)


Etymology

From Old Norse sokkr, from Latin soccus (“slipper”), from Ancient Greek σύκχος (súkkhos, “a kind of shoe”), probably from Phrygian or another language from Asia Minor.

Noun

socka c

  1. a sock (especially thicker or shorter ones)
