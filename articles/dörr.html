dörr (1760)


Etymology

From Old Swedish dyr, from Old Norse dyrr, from Proto-Germanic *durz, from Proto-Indo-European *dʰwer- (“door, gate”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /dœ̞rː/
  • Audio
    		(file)

Noun

dörr c

  1. door; portal of entry into a building or room, both the opening in a wall through which one can walk, and the rigid plane which may be used to temporarily close this opening
