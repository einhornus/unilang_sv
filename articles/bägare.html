bägare (11877)


Etymology

From Old Swedish bikare, from Old Norse bikarr (“cup”), from Old Saxon bikeri (“cup”), from Late Latin bīcārium (“wine vat, jug”). Related to German Becher.

Noun

bägare c

  1. a beaker, a mug, a cup, a drinking glass
    töm din bägare
    drink up (empty your cup)
