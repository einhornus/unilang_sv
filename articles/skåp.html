skåp (4842)


Etymology

From Old Swedish skap, from Middle Low German schap (cabinet, tub, bucket), from Old Saxon skap (tub), related to scoop. Cognate with Icelandic skápur.

Pronunciation

  • Audio

Noun

skåp n

  1. (furniture) a cabinet, a cupboard, a locker; a storage area which is not as large as a room, but which may be closed by a vertical door
