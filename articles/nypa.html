nypa (11447)


Etymology

From Old Swedish niupa, from Old Norse *hnjúpa, from Proto-Germanic *hneupaną (“to pluck”). Related to Gothic 𐌳𐌹𐍃𐌷𐌽𐌹𐌿𐍀𐌰𐌽 (dishniupan, “to break apart”).

Pronunciation

  • Audio
    		(file)

Noun

nypa c

  1. a pinch, a dash; a small amount of powder or granules
    tag en nypa salt
    add a dash of salt
  2. a grasp, a pinch; a firm grip with the fingers
    hon har starka nypor
    she has strong fingers; she is able to pinch quite hard
    hårda nypor
    a firm grip

Verb

nypa (present nyper, preterite nöp or nypte, supine nupit or nypt, imperative nyp)

  1. to pinch; to squeeze (between two fingers)
  2. (about frost and cold weather) to bite
