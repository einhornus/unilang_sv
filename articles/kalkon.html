kalkon (7626)


Etymology

Borrowed from Low German kalkûn or Dutch kalkoen, nominalisation of kalkûnsche höner or kalkoensche haan "hen from Calicut" (modern Kozhikode, India).[1][2]

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /kalˈkuːn/

Noun

kalkon c

  1. a turkey; a kind of large bird
  2. (colloquial) a turkey; a failure
