tji (73422)


Etymology

From Tavringer Romani tji (“not, nothing, no”), related to Traveller Norwegian kji (“not, nothing, no”), from Romani či (“not, nothing”). Probably a borrowing from Armenian չի (čʿi, “not, no”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ɕiː/
  • Rhymes: -ɕiː

Noun

tji (indeclinable)

  1. (colloquial) nothing, a backlash, an impossibility

Usage notes

  • Usually only found in certain expressions, such as få tji (“get nothing”).
