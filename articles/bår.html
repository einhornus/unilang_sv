bår (12109)


Etymology

From Old Norse bára, from Proto-Germanic *bērō, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰer-.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /boːr/
  • Homophone: bor

Noun

bår c

  1. a barrow, a litter (tool for carrying)
  2. a bier (carrying a dead person at a funeral)
  3. a stretcher; a simple litter designed to carry a sick, injured, or dead person.
