hänga (1514)


Etymology

From Old Norse hanga, from Proto-Germanic *hanhaną, From Proto-Indo-European *ḱonk-.

Pronunciation

  • audio
    		(file)

Verb

hänga (present hänger, preterite hängde, supine hängt, imperative häng)

  1. to hang
    1. to be suspended
    2. (colloquial) to loiter
    3. (computing, informal, reflexive) to stop responding
    4. to cause something to be suspended
    5. to place on a hook
    6. to execute by hanging
  2. (reflexive) to commit suicide by hanging
