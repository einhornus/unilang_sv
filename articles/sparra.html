sparra (40918)


Etymology

Possibly from Old Norse sperrask (“to kick out”).[1] However, possibly instead derived from Middle French esparer (“to kick”), from old Italian sparare (“to fling”), from Latin exparare, from ex- + parare (“to prepare”).[2][3] See also English parry.

Verb

sparra (present sparrar, preterite sparrade, supine sparrat, imperative sparra)

  1. To spar with someone (when training for boxing or martial arts).
