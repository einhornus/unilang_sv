klyfta (22902)


Etymology

From Old Norse kluft, klyft, from Proto-Germanic *kluftiz.

Noun

klyfta c

  1. a clove; a separate bulb of a garlic
  2. a wedge-shaped part of a fruit, either cut into shape or naturally formed (as with e.g. oranges)
  3. a cleft
  4. cleavage
