drake (3307)


Etymology

From Old Swedish draki, from Old Norse dreki, from Proto-Germanic *drakô (“dragon”), from Latin dracō (“serpent”), from Ancient Greek δράκων (drákōn, “dragon”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈdrɑːˌkɛ/
  • audio
    		(file)

Noun

drake c

  1. dragon
  2. kite
  3. a male duck, drake
  4. a belligerent (older) woman; battle-ax
