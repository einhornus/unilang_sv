gång (178)


Etymology

From Old Swedish ganger, from Old Norse gangr, from Proto-Germanic *gangaz.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ɡɔŋ/
  • audio
    		(file)

Noun

gång c

  1. walk, path, trail; a maintained, usually quite narrow path on which to walk, which in general does not follow next to a road. (Compare trottoar).
  2. (uncountable) manner of walk, gait
  3. (sports, uncountable) race walking
  4. (uncountable) the continuous running of something, such as a machinery or time.
  5. times; the number of times something happens
    Han gjorde det inte en gång, utan tre gånger
    He didn't do it once, but three times (thrice).
