Borrowed from English pie.

Etymology 2

Derived from paja (“to break”), from Tavringer Romani pagra, paggra (“to break, to destroy”), from Romani phag- (“to break, to destroy”).

Adjective

paj (comparative mer paj, superlative mest paj)

  1. (colloquial) broken; not working
    Bilen är helt paj.
    The car is completely broken.
