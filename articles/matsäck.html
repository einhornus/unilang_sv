matsäck (35850)


Etymology

From Old Swedish matsäkker. Equivalent of mat (“food”) + säck (“sack”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /²masɛk/
  • (spelling pronunciation) IPA(key): /²mɑːtˌsɛk/}

<span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_notes">Usage notesspan><span class="mw-editsection">span>

  • Matsäck can either be pronounced a short first vowel and no <span class="IPA">/t/span>, or with a long first vowel and an audible <span class="IPA">/t/span>. The first pronunciation is older and is an example of complete regressive assimilation, where clusters such as -ts- and -ds- were assimilated to -ss-, which also found in the pronunciation of words like ledsen, låtsas, midsommar, skjutsa, and many others. The second pronunciation is newer and have a reintroduced <span class="IPA">/t/span> due to spelling pronunciation. Both are considered standard and correct.
