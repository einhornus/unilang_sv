tråd (8386)


Etymology

From Old Norse þráðr (“thread”), from Proto-Germanic *þrēduz, from Proto-Indo-European *treh₁-tu-, from *terh₁- (“rub, twist”). Definition 3 is probably a calque of English thread.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈtroːd/
  • Audio
    		(file)

Noun

tråd c

  1. A thread (long, thin and flexible form of material).
  2. A thread, a continued theme or idea.
    Alla dessa essäer har en röd tråd.
    All of these essays have a common thread.
    Jag har tappat tråden av vad du säger.
    I’ve lost the thread of what you’re saying.
  3. (Internet) A thread, a series of messages, generally grouped by subject, in which all messages except the first are replies to previous messages in the thread
