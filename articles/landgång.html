landgång (72889)


Etymology

From land (“land, ground”) + gång (“path, place to walk”)

Noun

landgång c

  1. (nautical) gangplank; a board used as a temporary footbridge between a ship and a dockside
  2. a kind of long, narrow open sandwich, usually made by slicing a loaf of white bread length wise, with a topping consisting of e.g. boiled eggs, cold cuts, salad, shrimp, anchovies, cheese, ...
