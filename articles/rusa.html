rusa (6536)


Etymology

Related to ruse.

Perhaps from Proto-Germanic *hurskijaną (“to startle, drive”), from *hurskaz (“fast, rapid, quick”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱers- (“to run, hurry”).

Cognate with Old High German hurscan (“to speed, accelerate”), Old English horsc (“quick, quick-witted, clever”). More at hurry.

Pronunciation

  • audio

  • Rhymes: -²ʉːsa

Verb

rusa (present rusar, preterite rusade, supine rusat, imperative rusa)

  1. to rush, hurry
    Att rusa runt i panik.
    Rush round in panic.
