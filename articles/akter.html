akter (13954)


Etymology

From Middle Low German achter, from Old Saxon aftar, from Proto-Germanic *after. Cognate with Swedish efter, Dutch achter, English after.

Noun

akter c

  1. stern (the rear part or after end of a ship or vessel)
  2. (colloquial, humorous) bum, behind
  3. indefinite plural of akt
