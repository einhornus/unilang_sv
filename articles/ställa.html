ställa (875)


Etymology

From Old Swedish ställa, from Old Norse stilla (“to calm”), borrowed from Middle Low German stillen, from Old Saxon *stillian, from Proto-West Germanic *stillijan (“to make still”).

Pronunciation

  • Audio
    		(file)

Verb

ställa (present ställer, preterite ställde, supine ställt, imperative ställ)

  1. to set; to put something down in an upright position
  2. to set; adjust (a setting, or a clock)
  3. to make someone speechless with confusion

Usage notes

  • Often in the passive (constructed by means of the past participle): vara ställd: to be unable to answer; to be lost for words.
