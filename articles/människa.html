människa (847)


Etymology

From Old Swedish mænniskia, from Old Saxon mennisko, from the weak inflection of Proto-Germanic *manniskaz.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /mɛnːɪɧa/
  • audio
    		(file)

Noun

människa c

  1. a human being
  2. a person, people; (as a count)
    Tre människor skadades i olyckan.
    Three people were injured in the accident.
  3. (in plural) mankind
    Jag skulle ljuga, om jag sade, att jag älskar människorna.
    I should lie if I said that I love mankind.
