gnista (12025)


Etymology

From Old Norse gnist, from Proto-Germanic *gahnaistô (“spark”), from *ga- + *hnaistô (“spark”), perhaps from the ultimate source of German knistern (“to crackle”).[1]

Noun

gnista c

  1. spark; a small glowing particle
  2. spark; an electric discharge
