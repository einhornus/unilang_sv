häst (1233)


Etymology

From Old Swedish hæster, from Old Norse hestr, from Proto-Germanic *hangistaz, from Proto-Indo-European *ḱanḱest-, *kankest-.

Pronunciation

  • audio
    		(file)

Noun

häst c

  1. a horse; a large mammal, Equus caballus
    Han har många hästar i sitt stall.
    He has many horses in his stable.
    Synonyms: russ (dialectal), pålle (colloquial)
  2. (chess, colloquial) knight
    Jag ska flytta min häst.
    I'm going to move my knight.
    Synonym: springare
