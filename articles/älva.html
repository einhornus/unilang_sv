älva (15932)


Etymology

From Old Swedish ælf, from Old Norse alfr, from Proto-Germanic *albiz (“fairy, elf”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈɛlˌva/
  • audio
    		(file)
  • Homophone: elva

Noun

älva c

  1. A human-like creature from the Swedish mythology similar to an elf or fairy. She is often a young beautiful female, and one can sometimes see her dancing in the mist. Feminine form of alv.
