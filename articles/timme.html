timme (839)


Etymology

From Old Swedish tīme, from Old Norse tími, from Proto-Germanic *tīmô (“time”), from Proto-Germanic *tī-, from Proto-Indo-European *dī- (“time”).

Pronunciation

  • Audio
    		(file)

Noun

timme c

  1. an hour (time period of sixty minutes)
    • 1915, Dan Andersson, "Jag väntar...", Kolvaktarens visor
      Jag väntar vid min mila medan timmarna lida
      I'm tending my charcoal stack while the hours go by
  2. an hour, a time of day
    i denna sena timme
    this late
  3. an hour, a lesson, a class, a lecture, a period (in school, an item in the daily schedule)
    nästa timme har vi matte
    next hour is math
