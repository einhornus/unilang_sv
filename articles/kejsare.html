kejsare (5143)


Etymology

From Old Swedish kesar(e), keysar(e) (“emperor”), from Middle High German keiser, ultimately from Latin Caesar; the cognomen of Julius Caesar.[1]

Noun

kejsare c (feminine: kejsarinna)

  1. emperor (ruler of an empire)
