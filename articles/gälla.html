Etymology 1

From Old Swedish giælda, giælla, gælla, from Old Norse gjalda, from Proto-Germanic *geldaną, from Proto-Indo-European *gʰeldʰ- (“to pay”). Under influence from Low German gelden, compare gälda.

Verb

gälla (present gäller, preterite gällde, supine gällt, imperative gäll)

  1. to be valid; to be correct or proper
  2. to concern; to regard; to be about (something)
  3. to geld; to castrate
Usage notes
  • The first two meanings are by far the most common.
  • The geld meaning can also be conjugated -ade, according to gälla in Svenska Akademiens ordlista över svenska språket (8th ed., 1923)
