bröd (2270)


Etymology

From Old Swedish brø̄þ, from Old Norse brauð (“bread”), from Proto-Germanic *braudą. Cognate with Dutch brood (“bread”), German Brot (“bread”), English bread, Danish brød (“bread”), Icelandic brauð (“bread”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /brøː(d)/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Rhymes: -øːd, -øː

Noun

bröd n

  1. (countable) bread

Usage notes

  • Since bröd is sometimes pronounced /brøː/ (without the final /d/), it is sometimes treated as a vowel final neuter word like spö (“rod”) with the definite singular bröt, plural brön, definite plural bröna, compare träd.
