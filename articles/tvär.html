tvär (27692)


Etymology

From Old Swedish þvar, þvær, from Old Norse þverr, from Proto-Germanic *þwerhaz, presumably from Proto-Indo-European *terkʷ-.

Pronunciation

  • Rhymes: -æːr

Adjective

tvär

  1. across, perpendicular to the main direction
  2. sharp (of a curve)
  3. sudden, immediate
  4. stubborn, grumpy (of a person who gives short, sharp answers that oppose what most would think)
