gyttja (27612)


Etymology

Probably from Proto-Germanic *gutjōn, related to *geutaną (“to pour”).[1]

Noun

gyttja c

  1. watery mud, muck
  2. (geology) a fine-grained, nutrient-rich organic mud, or peat, deposited in lakes and ponds
