lägg (331)


Etymology

From Old Swedish lægger, from Old Norse leggr, from Proto-Germanic *lagjaz, from Proto-Indo-European *(ǝ)lak-, *lēk-.

Noun

lägg c

  1. (dated) a leg
  2. (cooking) knuckle, shin, hand; part of a leg of an animal

lägg n

  1. a file; a printed sheet that becomes a number of pages in a book
