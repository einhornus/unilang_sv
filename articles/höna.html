höna (13148)


Etymology

From Old Swedish hø̄na, from Old Norse hœna, from Proto-Germanic *hōnijǭ, related to *hanjō, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *kan- (“to sing”), *kana-.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /²høːna/, [²høə̯n̪a]
  • audio
    		(file)
  • Rhymes: -²øːna

Noun

höna c

  1. hen; in particular a female chicken
    Hönor äter maskar.
    Hens eat worms.
