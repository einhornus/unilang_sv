Etymology 2

From Old Swedish næra, from Middle Low German neren, from Old Saxon nerian, from Proto-Germanic *nazjaną (“to save; rescue; heal”), from *nesaną (“to survive, recover, heal”) + *-janą (causative).

Verb

nära (present när, preterite närde, supine närt, imperative när)

  1. to nourish, nurture; give nourishment to
  2. to nurture; keep alive (such as a dream)
