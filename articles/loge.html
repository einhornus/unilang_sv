loge (14921)


Etymology 1

From French loge, from Old French, from Frankish *laubija (“arbour, protective roof, shelter made of foliage”), related to Old High German louppea, from Proto-Germanic *laubijǭ, from Proto-Germanic *laubą (“leaf; folliage”), probably from Proto-Indo-European *lewbʰ- (“to break, cut off, peel off, flake off, shed”).

Pronunciation 1

  • IPA(key): /ˈloːɧ/

Noun

loge c

  1. A backstage dressing room for actors at a theatre.
  2. A private seating chamber at a theatre.
  3. A section or local chapter of an order (for instance freemasons).
