hedersknyffel (72289)


Etymology

From heder + -s- + knyffel, used since 1834. The word knyffel possibly originates from karnyffel/karnibel/karnobel (“a mean person”), from Danish karniffel and German Karnüffel, a card game and the jack in a deck of cards.

Noun

hedersknyffel c

  1. a jolly good fellow, a regular trump, a realy nice person
    Hör nu, hedersknyfflar, vi äro f. n. i en svår klämma.
    Listen now, my dear friends, we are currently in a very dire situation.
