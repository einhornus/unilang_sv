bänk (12320)


Etymology

From Old Swedish bænker, from (East) Old Norse *benkr (cf. West Old Norse bekkr), from Proto-Germanic *bankiz,.

Pronunciation

  • Audio
    		(file)

Noun

bänk c

  1. bench, a long sofa without bolster
  2. bench; where players of a sport sit when not playing
  3. workbench
  4. school desk; a very simple table - possibly with a sloping top - for the use of students and school pupils
  5. kitchen bench
