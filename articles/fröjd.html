fröjd (13710)


Etymology

From Old Swedish fryghþ, frygh, frøgdh, frøghdh, frøgd, frøghd, from Old Norse frygð, from Proto-Norse *ᚠᚱᛁᚷᚷᚹᛁᚦᛟ (*friggwiþō-), from Proto-Germanic *friwiþō; or possibly from *ᚠᚱᚢᚷᚷᚹᛁᚦᛟ (*fruggwiþō-), from Proto-Germanic *fruwiþō, both ultimately from Proto-Germanic *frawaz.[1] Cognate with Elfdalian fröjð, Norwegian Nynorsk frygd, Danish fryd (whence also Norwegian Bokmål fryd and Norwegian Nynorsk fryd), Icelandic frygð and also German Freude which has influenced the modern meaning of the word in all Nordic languages.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈfrœjd/
  • Audio
    		(file)

Noun

fröjd c

  1. glee, happiness, joy, pleasure, merriment; mirth; gayety
    Här är allt frid och fröjd, ingen anledning till oro.
    Here everything is peace and joy, no reason for worry.
    Det har varit en fröjd att få spendera den här middagen med dig.
    It has been a pleasure to spend this dinner with you.
    Synonyms: nöje, glädje
