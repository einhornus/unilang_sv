öra (3463)


Etymology

From Old Swedish ø̄ra, from Old Norse eyra, from the voiced Verner alternant of Proto-Germanic *ausô, from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ows-.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /øːra/, [œ̞ːra]
  • audio
    		(file)

Noun

öra n

  1. (anatomy) ear; the part of the body used to hear
  2. ear; the handle of a cup, designed not to get warm when a hot liquid is poured in to the cup
