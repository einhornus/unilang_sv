knyta (6966)


Etymology

From Old Swedish knyta, from Old Norse knýta, from Proto-Germanic *knuttijaną, related to *knuttô.

Verb

knyta (present knyter, preterite knöt, supine knutit, imperative knyt)

  1. to tie (a knot)
  2. (reflexive, idiomatic) to go to sleep
