From Old Norse bringa.

Etymology 2

Borrowed from Middle Low German bringen, brengen, from Old Saxon brengian, bringan, from Proto-Germanic *bringaną, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰrenk-. Compare Dutch brengen, German bringen, English bring, West Frisian bringe.

Pronunciation

  • audio
    		(file)

Verb

bringa (present bringar, preterite bringade or bragte, supine bringat or bragt, imperative bringa)

  1. (archaic) to bring; to transport toward someone else
  2. (archaic) to get someone to do something
